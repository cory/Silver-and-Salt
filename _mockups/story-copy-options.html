Our Story

I built a company in Utah. Then I came back to change who funds them.

Silver & Salt Capital started with one founder's promise — and a hundred women ready to keep it.

Let's dream together about what's possible.

In 2022, I was running a startup in Salt Lake City with one of my best friends. We had a real business — a private equity roll-up strategy in home services, a plan to acquire and consolidate nationally. We weren't building a side project. We were building something we believed could scale.

To raise capital, I did what founders do here. I joined SheMoney, How Women Succeed, Founders N' Fuders. We got into the Kinect Capital accelerator. We pitched at WeROC and RevRoad. I met incredible women, found real champions, and built friendships that still shape my life. Two women wrote large checks because they understood what we were building. One man did because he'd experienced our services firsthand.

But for every investor who caught the vision, there were dozens of conversations that went nowhere — with men who couldn't see the value in what we were offering. One told me, to my face, that I "did not look like the kind of person who runs a company."

We ultimately chose not to pursue the national expansion, and the business still operates profitably in Salt Lake today. It's a good outcome. But I left knowing that the fundraising experience I'd had wasn't unique to me — it was structural.

I made a promise to myself: I would come back to Utah and make this journey better for the next wave of women founders.

One of my early startups had an exit. And now I'm home. Silver & Salt Capital is that promise kept — a community of women who are done waiting for invitations into rooms that were never built for us. We're building our own. And we're funding the founders that this ecosystem has been overlooking.

This is just the beginning. Come build with us.

Our Story

Utah has the capital. It has the founders. What it's missing is the connection between them.

Silver & Salt Capital exists to close that gap — starting with 100 women who invest together.

The gap is real. So is the opportunity.

I've been a startup CEO, a fundraiser, and a founder who navigated the Utah investor landscape firsthand. I spent two years building a home services company with a private equity roll-up strategy — a real business with a national expansion plan. The company still operates profitably in Salt Lake City today.

During that time, I went through the full Utah ecosystem: SheMoney, How Women Succeed, Founders N' Fuders, the Kinect Capital accelerator, pitch competitions at WeROC and RevRoad. The community here is strong. The women are extraordinary. The networks are real.

What's missing is the capital infrastructure. I sat across from dozens of investors who couldn't see past their own pattern recognition. The women who invested understood the business immediately. The majority of men I pitched did not. The gap between who has capital and who understands what deserves it — that's the opportunity.

Utah ranks #3 in the nation to start a business. It ranks #50 for women's equality. Less than 6% of the state's pre-seed and seed capital reaches startups with a female founder. This isn't a pipeline problem. It's a funding problem. And funding problems get solved by funders.

Silver & Salt Capital is 100 accredited women investing together — sourcing deals, sharing diligence, deploying capital individually but learning collectively. We're modeled after proven networks like Golden Seeds and The JumpFund. This approach works. It just hasn't existed in Utah — until now.

The first 100 are forming now.

Our Story

The fundraising experience that changed everything — and the community it created.

How one founder's journey through Utah's capital landscape became the blueprint for Silver & Salt Capital.

It started with a startup. It became a movement.

When I launched a startup in Salt Lake City in 2022, I wasn't thinking about building an angel network. I was thinking about growth — a PE roll-up strategy, national expansion, operational efficiency at scale. My co-founder was one of my best friends. The business was real.

Raising capital taught me something the pitch deck couldn't. The women I met — through SheMoney, How Women Succeed, Kinect Capital, WeROC, RevRoad — were extraordinary. Sharp, generous, connected. Two wrote large checks immediately. They saw what we were building because they'd built things themselves.

The men were a different story. Dozens of conversations where the value of our work simply didn't register. And one investor who told me plainly that I "did not look like the kind of person who runs a company."

I chose not to pursue the national expansion, and the business continues to operate profitably in Salt Lake today. But I left with a clear understanding: the problem isn't the founders. It's who holds the capital and how they deploy it.

After an exit from an earlier startup, I came back to Utah with a simple question: what if the women I'd met — the executives, the operators, the professionals with real capital — what if we stopped waiting for seats at someone else's table and built our own?

That question became Silver & Salt Capital. A hundred women, investing together, with the deal flow and diligence infrastructure to do it well. Not charity. Not activism. A market correction — led by the people this market has been underestimating.

We're not waiting anymore. Neither should you.
