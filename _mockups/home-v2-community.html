What's an accredited investor?

It's a legal term. It means you meet certain income or wealth standards that let you invest in startups — companies that aren't on the stock market yet. The government requires this because startup investing is riskier than buying public stocks.

If you meet any one of the criteria on the right, you qualify.

Not sure? That's okay. We can help you figure it out.

You Qualify If
You earn $200K+ per year on your own
You and your spouse earn $300K+ per year together
Your net worth is over $1 million (not counting your home)
You hold certain financial licenses (Series 7, 65, or 82)
