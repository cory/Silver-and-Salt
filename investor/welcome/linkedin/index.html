Add Silver & Salt to your LinkedIn

Your LinkedIn tells founders who you are and what you back. Adding Silver & Salt Capital builds credibility, brings deal flow to our community, and makes the collective visible to the people we want to find us.

Step One

Choose how to add it.

Two standard placements — pick whichever fits your profile narrative. Experience reads more professional; Volunteer Experience reads more community-driven. Either works.

Option A
Add as Experience

Treats your membership like a professional role. Appears in your main Experience timeline.

  1. Go to your LinkedIn profile. In the Experience section, click the + button, then Add position.
  2. Fill in the fields using the suggestions below.
  3. Paste one of the description templates and save.
Suggested Fields
TitleFounding Member — or: Investor, Angel Investor
CompanySilver & Salt Capital
TypePart-time
LocationYour city, Utah (or "Remote")
Start dateThe month you joined
Option B
Add as Volunteer Experience

Treats your membership as community involvement. Appears in a separate section further down your profile.

  1. On your profile, click Add profile section.
  2. Under Additional, choose Add volunteer experience.
  3. Fill in the fields, paste a description, and save.
Suggested Fields
OrganizationSilver & Salt Capital
RoleInvestor — or: Angel Investor, Founding Member
CauseEconomic Empowerment
Start dateThe month you joined
Step Two

Copy a description.

Three on-brand options — pick the one that matches how you want to show up. Tap Copy and paste into the Description field.

Short
1 line — great for the headline or a brief role
Founding member of Silver & Salt Capital — Utah's first women's angel investment collective, connecting capital to Utah founders who use it best.
Standard
3–4 sentences — the default
Silver & Salt Capital is Utah's first women's angel investment collective — a community of accredited women who vet opportunities together and individually back high-potential Utah founders. Members commit $10,000+ per year for five years, building a diversified portfolio of 30+ Utah companies founded by women through shared diligence and individual decisions. Deals are structured as SPVs on a syndicate platform, so individual checks become a single, clean line on the founder's cap table. Women-founded companies return more revenue per dollar invested, yet rarely see capital. We exist to connect capital to the Utah founders who use it best.
Founding Cohort
Emphasizes the first-100 origin story
I'm a founding member of Silver & Salt Capital — Utah's first women's angel investment collective. Alongside 100 accredited women, I vet deals with my community and individually fund Utah's highest-potential founders. Our thesis: women-founded companies deliver stronger returns per dollar invested, yet rarely see capital. We're building the infrastructure to change that — connecting capital to the Utah founders who use it best, one founder at a time.
