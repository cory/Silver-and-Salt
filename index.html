Let's dream together about what's possible.

In 2022, I served as CEO of a startup based in Salt Lake City for 2 years. While launching a company with one of my best friends, we explored a private equity roll-up strategy to acquire companies like ours nationwide and consolidate operations to maximize operational efficiency and profitability.

To connect with other women and investors in Utah, I joined SheMoney, How Women Succeed, Founders N' Fuders. We joined the Kinect Capital accelerator, and we entered two pitch competitions for WeROC and RevRoad. I met so many amazing friends and loved all the support and robust network. We found some lovely champions. We had large check commitments from 2 women who understood the value of our work and one man who experienced our home services and caught the vision.

While seeking investors, I had dozens of conversations with male investors who did not understand the value of the home services we were offering — and one who bluntly told me I "did not look like the kind of person who runs a company."

For a myriad of reasons, we ultimately decided not to pursue a national expansion and we did not take capital to grow. Today, the business continues to successfully operate in the SLC region as a profitable lifestyle business run by my talented and amazing friend.

As I moved on to other startups, I promised myself that I would return to my home state of Utah at another point in my career to see if I could make this journey better for the next wave of women founders.

One of my early startups had an exit, and I'm back exploring interest among my friends and our broader community to launch a women's angel network in Utah. We can make it easier for women founders in this state to raise pre- and seed-stage capital.

Why Silver & Salt?

Utah is one of the best places in America to build a business[S01] — and still one of the hardest places to be a woman building one.[S02]

The problem is not talent. The problem is not ambition. The problem is participation and access to growth capital. This group unites women in the Utah region with a shared purpose: to help new women founders launch and lead successful businesses in our community.

Capital exists here. Experience exists here. Influence exists here. We know that despite all of this, many women remain outside the networks where investment decisions are made.

Silver & Salt Capital represent two minerals that shaped the Park City and Wasatch region. At times, they've both been more valuable than gold, and they represent the resources we have to shape a new landscape for women founders today.

And yes, if we have a few silver hairs or are just a little more salty than one might expect, it's all a part of what makes our Utah community so strong and vibrant.

The Opportunity

Two sides of one equation — still unconnected

129,429
women-owned businesses in Utah — up from 31% to 44.5% in four years5

Thousands in the $250K–$5M range. Profitable. Growing. Creating jobs. Too big for friends and family, wrong fit for venture capital. Almost nobody is writing them checks.

~25,000
accredited women investors in the Salt Lake–Park City corridor

Women with the means to invest in early-stage businesses. Most have never been invited to an angel table — not because they're not qualified, but because nobody built the on-ramp.

Connect these two sides and you get more businesses funded, more jobs created, better returns. That's not aspiration. That's math.

The capital system wasn't built against women. It just wasn't built for them yet. Utah's angel ecosystem grew out of SaaS exits through technical founder networks. That's not a conspiracy — it's how ecosystems form. And it means an enormous segment of high-performing founders has been structurally locked out.

Women in Utah are disproportionately building in health, wellness, education, childcare, consumer services, and professional services — the exact categories the youngest, fastest-growing state in the country needs most. These aren't niche businesses. They're responses to structural shortages.

Utah's Paradox

#3 to start a business. #50 for women's equality.

That's not a contradiction. That's a pricing error. Every ingredient for business success exists here — low taxes, fast-growth economy, deep talent, regulatory ease. But the capital infrastructure was built by tech founders investing in other tech founders. The gap between #3 and #50 is where the returns live.
