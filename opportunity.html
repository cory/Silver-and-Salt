Utah isn't capital-constrained. It's participation-constrained.

The Problem

The same gap, seen from two angles.

Utah's startup ecosystem is world-class. But look at who's participating in it — on either side of the table — and a clear pattern emerges. Women are building companies. Women have capital to invest. But the systems that connect them have been built by and for someone else.

Side One — The Founders

Women are building
extraordinary companies.

Utah has 129,429 women-owned businesses. Women founders consistently outperform on revenue efficiency, ROI, and capital discipline. The performance case is not in dispute.

And yet: all-female founding teams received just 1% of U.S. venture capital in 2024. In Utah specifically, we estimate only 1.92% of VC funding has gone to female founders. The problem is not quality. It is access.

Side Two — The Investors

Women have capital.
They need the on-ramp.

There are an estimated ~100K accredited women investors in the Salt Lake–Park City corridor. Women collectively control $10.9 trillion in investable assets nationally and are on track to control $30 trillion by 2030.

The capital is here. What's been missing is the trusted, accessible on-ramp. The community where women can learn together, evaluate deals together, and invest with confidence — without having to walk into a room where they're the only one.

The Model

A coordinated community. Individual decisions.

Silver & Salt Capital is not a fund. No one gives us their money to manage. Every member makes her own investment decisions — with the benefit of shared deal flow, shared diligence, shared relationships, and shared knowledge.

The collective amplifies every individual. When 50 women evaluate the same deal, the quality of diligence goes up. When 50 women back the same founder, the network effect compounds. That's the model.

We commit $10,000+ individually and move $1M+ collectively. And we're just getting started.
