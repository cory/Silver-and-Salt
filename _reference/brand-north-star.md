# Silver & Salt Capital — Brand North Star

## Website Goals (Priority Order)

### 1. Make the movement feel inevitable.
This is the emotional core. Before she understands the model, before she sees the data, she should feel that something important is forming in Utah and that the women who get in early will shape it. The site should never read like a pitch. It should read like a dispatch from something already underway. *If she leaves feeling "that sounds nice," we've failed. If she leaves feeling "I don't want to miss this," we've won.*

### 2. Make the model undeniable.
She controls her money. Every dollar, every decision, hers. $10,000/year into companies she selects. Over 5 years, a diversified portfolio of 30+ investments. That's how individual agency creates collective impact — not by pooling money into someone else's fund, but by hundreds of women making informed, independent decisions in the same direction. *The power is in the structure, not the pitch.*

### 3. Earn trust through substance.
Trust is not a section of the site — it's the texture of every sentence. We don't claim credibility; we demonstrate it. Published research with sources. Transparent fees with no fine print. A founder who has done this before and came back to build infrastructure. The visitor should never have to wonder "is this legitimate?" — the answer should be self-evident from the rigor of the work. *Show, never tell.*

### 4. Arm her with conviction.
She should leave the site smarter than when she arrived. The performance data, the market gap, the Utah paradox — she should be able to explain this opportunity to her spouse, her financial advisor, her friends over dinner. We're not asking her to take a leap of faith. We're giving her the evidence to take a calculated step. *An informed investor is a confident one.*

### 5. Compel her to act now.
The founding cohort is forming. This is a specific, limited moment — not an open-ended invitation. The women who join now will set the investment thesis, shape the culture, and be the foundation of everything that follows. Waiting means watching. *Founding members don't join later.*

## What We Will NOT Do

These are brand guardrails. If the copy ever drifts toward any of these, it's wrong.

**We will not plead.**
We don't need anyone's sympathy. The data makes the case. We present an opportunity — we don't ask for favors. No "please consider" energy. No "we need your help" framing. The founders need capital and the investors need returns. We built the bridge. Walk across it or don't.

**We will not guilt.**
The gender funding gap is real, but shame is not our tool. We will never frame investing as obligation, charity, or penance. If the only reason someone joins is guilt, she's the wrong member. The reason to join is that this is a *smart investment* that also builds something meaningful.

**We will not hedge.**
No "we hope to" or "we aspire to" or "if all goes well." Hedging signals doubt. We state what we're building, what the data shows, and what the plan is. Where there's genuine risk (angel investing is risky), we name it plainly — that's honesty, not hedging. But we don't apologize for our ambition.

**We will not sound like a nonprofit.**
Silver & Salt is an investment collective. The language should reflect financial sophistication, not cause marketing. No "giving back," no "making a difference," no "supporting women." We are deploying capital into high-performing founders for returns. The social impact is a consequence of good investing, not the other way around.

**We will not infantilize.**
No "girl boss." No "ladies." No pink. No diminutives. No cutesy financial metaphors. These are accredited investors making serious capital allocation decisions. The voice should respect their intelligence and experience at every turn.

**We will not be vague about money.**
Every fee disclosed. Every structure explained. Every number sourced. If she has to guess how her money works, we've failed. Financial clarity is not a nice-to-have — it's the foundation of trust through substance.

**We will not oversell returns.**
Angel investing is high-risk. We will never imply guaranteed outcomes. The portfolio math (30+ companies, diversification) improves odds, and the performance data on women founders is real — but we will always frame this as informed risk-taking, not a sure thing. Overpromising destroys the trust we're building.

**We will not sound like a pitch deck.**
No "TAM/SAM/SOM." No "disrupting." No "10x." This is a human organization doing human work with real money. The voice is warm, direct, and grounded — not a Series A presentation to a room of VCs.
