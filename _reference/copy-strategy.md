# Silver & Salt Capital — Copy Strategy Brief

## Target Audience

**Primary:** Accredited women investors in the Salt Lake–Park City corridor

**Profile:**
- High-net-worth women — professionals, entrepreneurs, executives, inheritors
- Age range likely 35–60
- Financially literate but most have NOT done angel investing before
- Currently investing in public markets, real estate, maybe some PE/VC funds
- Connected in the Utah business community
- Drawn to both returns AND impact — but won't invest on mission alone

**What they want:**
- Access to deal flow they can't find on their own
- Confidence they're making smart bets (not charity)
- Community of sharp, like-minded women
- A way to participate in Utah's startup economy that's been male-dominated
- Structure and guidance — they don't want to figure out angel investing solo

**What holds them back:**
- Unfamiliarity with angel investing mechanics and risk profile
- "Is this real?" — skepticism about new, unproven organizations
- Time commitment uncertainty
- Not knowing if they're "the right fit"
- Fear of losing money on something that sounds idealistic

---

## Voice & Tone

**Lead with:** Confident authority. This is smart money, not a cause.

**Register:** Premium, composed, precise. Think Goldman Sachs private wealth meets Patagonia mission clarity. Never preachy. Never apologetic. Never "girl boss."

**Rules:**
- State facts, don't plead. The data is the argument.
- Use "we" to signal insider status, not sales pitch.
- Short sentences for impact. Longer ones only when the complexity demands it.
- No jargon without context. No buzzwords ("synergy," "ecosystem," "empower").
- "Women founders outperform" is a market thesis, not a social statement. Frame it that way.

---

## Key Messages (priority order)

### 1. The Performance Case
Women founders generate more revenue per dollar invested, burn less capital, and deliver stronger returns. This isn't advocacy — it's alpha.

### 2. The Market Gap
Utah has 129,000+ women-owned businesses and ~100,000 accredited women investors. Almost none of that capital is reaching those founders. That's not a social problem — it's a market inefficiency.

### 3. The Power of Coordinated Capital
Alone, you're writing checks. Together, you're a force — better deal flow, shared diligence, collective leverage, lower risk per person.

### 4. The Founding Moment
The first cohort is forming now. This is the ground floor of something that doesn't exist in Utah yet.

---

## Primary Value Differentiators

### 1. Movement That Transforms Utah's Women Founder Ecosystem
This isn't an investment club — it's infrastructure that doesn't exist yet. When hundreds of women coordinate capital, deal flow, mentorship, and connections, the entire ecosystem shifts. More capital flowing to women founders means more women start companies, more women get funded, more women succeed — a flywheel that permanently changes Utah. Every member isn't just making investments; she's building the system her daughters will inherit.

### 2. Individual Investment Decisions + Portfolio Math That Works
Every woman makes her own investment decisions — no blind pool, no fund manager deciding for you. The math: commit $10,000/year, spread across multiple companies, and over 5 years you build a portfolio of 30+ companies. That's real diversification. Angel investing is risky company by company, but a diversified portfolio of 30+ investments following this thesis has strong expected returns — especially when you're investing in the most capital-efficient founders in the market.

## Additional Differentiators

### vs. Investing Alone
- Curated deal flow (you don't have to find deals)
- Shared due diligence (you don't have to vet alone)
- Community intelligence (learn from other investors' experience)
- Collective leverage (better terms, more founder access)

### vs. Traditional VC Funds
- You choose your investments (not a blind pool)
- You keep your capital until you deploy it
- You're part of the community, not a passive LP
- Lower minimums, transparent fees

### vs. Other Angel Groups
- Utah-focused (local market knowledge, local network effects)
- Women-led and women-focused (aligned thesis, not a side initiative)
- Research-driven (published, open-source research — we show our work)
- Founding stage (shape the culture and direction)

## Page Strategy

### Home
**Job:** Hook with the opportunity, establish credibility, drive to application.
**Arc:** Arresting stat → what we are (one line) → performance case (brief) → the gap → CTA

### About
**Job:** Build trust through founder story and organizational clarity.
**Arc:** Who we are → how it works (overview) → founder story → commitments

### Opportunity
**Job:** Make the market gap feel urgent and investable.
**Arc:** Two numbers (businesses + investors) → the disconnect → why Utah, why now → CTA

### The Data
**Job:** Arm investors with the performance case. Make it undeniable.
**Arc:** Headline stat → supporting data → link to full research → CTA

### How It Works
**Job:** Demystify angel investing and explain the S&S model clearly.
**Arc:** The model (learn, vet, invest) → what you commit → what you get → fees → timeline

### Join
**Job:** Close. Make the application feel like an invitation, not a form.
**Arc:** You've seen the data, you know the gap → here's what founding members get → requirements → apply

## Headline Direction

Current headlines are clever but sometimes opaque. New direction: **clear over clever.** Every headline should be immediately understood and create forward momentum.

Examples of the shift:

| Current | Proposed Direction |
|---|---|
| "129,000 businesses need capital. 100,000 investors need deal flow. Nobody built the room." | "Utah's best-performing founders can't find capital. Utah's smartest investors can't find them." |
| "Two sides of one equation — still unconnected" | "129,000 women-owned businesses. $509M in startup capital. Almost none of it connecting." |
| "Women founders outperform. The capital hasn't noticed." | "Women founders generate 78 cents per dollar invested. Men generate 31. The market hasn't caught up." |
| "Simple. Powerful. Yours." | "How It Works" (let the content be powerful — the headline just needs to orient) |
| "The math gets fun fast" | "Join the Founding Cohort" |

## Graphics & Visual Strategy

The current site uses no images — all CSS/SVG. To increase credibility and warmth:

1. **Data visualizations** — Simple, elegant charts showing the performance gap and market opportunity. Not infographics — think Financial Times style.
2. **The S&S model diagram** — A clean visual showing the learn → vet → invest cycle
3. **Utah market map** — Geographic visualization of the opportunity
4. **Comparison graphics** — Side-by-side performance data (women vs. market)
5. **Social proof elements** — As the cohort forms, member count / logos / quotes
